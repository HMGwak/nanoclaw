# Debate Modes (Preserved)

This document preserves mode-level behavior patterns for structured
multi-agent discussions.

## Standard

- Free round-robin discussion.
- Goal: reach a practical convergence with minimal ceremony.

## Oxford

- Formal pro/con framing.
- Goal: compare two clear positions under explicit burden of proof.

## Advocate

- One explicit challenger role critiques the majority path.
- Goal: stress-test assumptions and identify failure points early.

## Socratic

- Question-led progression that repeatedly probes assumptions.
- Goal: expose hidden premises before committing to a plan.

## Delphi

- Iterative estimate/revision cycles with convergence checks.
- Goal: tighten uncertainty for forecasting, effort, or risk.

## Brainstorm

- Divergent ideation first, convergence second.
- Goal: maximize option discovery before evaluation.

## Tradeoff

- Criteria-driven comparison with explicit scoring.
- Goal: make decisions auditable across competing priorities.
